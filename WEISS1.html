How much purchase tax do foreign buyers pay in Israel?

As of 2026, non-resident and investor buyers in Israel pay 8% purchase tax (Mas Rechisha) on the property value up to approximately ₪6,055,070 and 10% on any amount above that threshold. New immigrants (olim chadashim) qualify for sharply reduced brackets. Weiss Group's tax advisors confirm the exact figure for every client.

What fees do I pay when buying real estate in Israel?

Beyond the property price: 8–10% purchase tax (non-resident), 0.5–1.5% legal fees + 18% VAT, 2% broker fee + VAT (if applicable), ₪2,500–₪5,000 appraisal, and minor notary/registration costs. Our interactive calculator above gives you the exact breakdown.

Can I buy property in Israel from abroad without visiting?

Yes. Weiss Group handles the full process remotely — virtual tours, Power of Attorney, secure electronic signatures, and a single escort coordinating every specialist. Most foreign clients complete their transaction without leaving home country, though we always welcome a visit for the handover.

Why invest in Israeli real estate in 2026?

Israel has delivered exceptional long-term price growth, features one of the strongest land-registry (Tabu) systems in the world, enjoys sustained population growth, and offers strong rental yields in key metros. For the Jewish diaspora, it also secures a legacy, an aliyah landing pad, and a tangible connection to heritage. Foreign buyer mortgage volume has more than doubled in the past two years.

Does Weiss Group provide legal and tax advice directly?

Yes — independent, senior real-estate lawyers and tax advisors are part of Weiss Group's dedicated foreign-buyer network. They represent your interests and are fluent in cross-border transactions (US, UK, EU, and more).

Can I get an Israeli mortgage as a non-resident?

Yes. Non-residents typically qualify for mortgages up to 50% loan-to-value at Israeli banks. Weiss Group has direct relationships with the country's leading banks and coordinates the mortgage process on your behalf.

What if I start as a foreign buyer and make aliyah during the deal?

This actually works in your favor. If your aliyah is recognized before closing (Teudat Oleh issued), you may qualify for the sharply reduced olim purchase tax brackets — potentially saving tens of thousands of shekels. Our tax advisors structure the timing of your purchase to maximize this benefit when appropriate.

Can my children inherit an Israeli property I buy as a foreign resident?

Yes. Israeli real estate held by foreign nationals can be inherited by heirs worldwide under Israel's inheritance laws. Weiss Group coordinates with Israeli estate attorneys to structure ownership (personal, trust, LLC) for optimal cross-border inheritance and estate-tax treatment in your home country.

Is it safe to invest in Israeli real estate after October 7?

Israeli real estate has historically shown remarkable resilience through every geopolitical cycle. Since October 2023, foreign buyer mortgage volume has more than doubled — reflecting the combination of diaspora demand, Israel's long-term fundamentals, and the strength of the Tabu title system. Weiss Group focuses on metro markets (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, central cities) with the deepest buyer pool and strongest liquidity.

Are there countries Weiss Group cannot accept funds from?

Yes — Weiss Group is fully compliant with Israel's Prohibition on Money Laundering Law (2000) and international sanctions regimes (OFAC, EU, UK). Every transaction undergoes KYC verification and Source of Funds documentation. If you have questions about your jurisdiction, our compliance team provides a confidential preliminary review.

How does the Community Foundation Contribution work for rabbis who refer buyers?

Weiss Group operates an ethical, legally compliant community-partnership model. When a buyer from a partner community closes a purchase, Weiss makes a tax-deductible contribution to the community institution (the Chabad house, synagogue, or educational foundation) — never a personal commission to the rabbi. The funds support mikvah, education, scholarship, and community programs. This structure complies with Israeli brokerage law, US RESPA, and EU GDPR rules, and is confirmed by independent legal counsel.

What property-management options exist if I don't plan to live in the property?

Weiss Group offers full diaspora property management — long-term rentals, short-term holiday lets (for families visiting during chagim), tenant screening, rent collection, maintenance, and annual financial reporting. Management fees typically range 5–8% of rental income. Many clients cover their mortgage entirely from rental yield.
